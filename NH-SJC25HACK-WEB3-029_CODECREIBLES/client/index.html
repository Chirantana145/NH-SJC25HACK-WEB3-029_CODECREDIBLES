üìò Learn More: Understanding MEV

Front-running

When an attacker sees your pending transaction in the public mempool and places a faster, similar transaction *just before* yours to profit from your trade.

Back-running

When an attacker places a transaction *right after* yours to gain an advantage, usually by taking advantage of a price change or other market effect caused by your transaction.

Sandwich Attack

An attacker places one transaction *before* yours and one *after* yours, manipulating the price and profiting from your trade.

Live Attack Feed

A real-time, simulated monitor that tracks suspicious transaction patterns and flags potential attacker activity.

Transaction Simulator

A tool to predict and test the likelihood of your intended trade being targeted by an MEV attack before you actually execute the transaction.

Private Mempool

A secure, protected environment that hides your transactions from the public mempool to prevent MEV attacks.
